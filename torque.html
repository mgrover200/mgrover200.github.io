CYLINDER HEAD TORQUE SETTINGS

SERVICES AND MAINTENANCE ROVER 200 25 ZR

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

The surfaces of engine block and cylinder head were each meticulously cleaned, before the new gasket is fitted, each of the 10 cylinder head bolts were then inserted back into their respective holes. The head bolts require a size 12 torx bit, prior to inserting the head bolts, oil was applied to the cleaned bolt threads, and under each head bolt collar before tightened in the correct sequence.

CYLINDER HEAD TIGHTENING

Stage (1) 20Nm or 15 lbf ft Stage (2) angle tighten through 180 degrees. Stage (3) angle tighten finally through another 180 degrees. Cylinder head gasket used was LVB-0000320.

CAM CARRIER TIGHTENING

Camshaft's bearing carrier's 26 x 10mm bolts, were then torqued to the shown specific order of 1 to 26 to just 10Nm or 7lb ft. with silicone sealant applied, engine top cover was then fitted back with the 15 x 8mm bolts, torqued in sequence to 9Nm or 6lb ft.

ENGINE COVER TIGHTENING

Prior to fitting the cam or engine top cover this required a new cam cover gasket fitted, this goes on dry with no sealant added, but needs to be fitted the correct way round its already marked up. Cam cover gasket LDR-102000 was used for this project.

At this stage, and with a good lubrication of engine oil applied to each of the cam bearing surfaces before assembly, regarded as good practice prior to the engines initial first start up. We now have the oil filter, and a correct amount of engine oil to be added. .

OIL FILTER FITTING INFORMATION

FITTING PART NUMBER LPW-100181   

The oil filter we used at this time, was the LPW-100181 some suppliers may also include with the filter, a new sump plug washer. Our removal tool during this process was Draper oil filter wrench OFW-100.   .

Fitting back the new oil filter, without it first being  primed is bad news. As the engine will be starved of lubrication during the critical few seconds, it will take before the oil filter is filled and is ready to begin circulating oil around the engine. A good method is to first add oil to the filter, and then  rotate it several times by hand holding it at a slight angle, then top up again, should hold at least 150 ml without any spillage when fitting back.

Then its a smear of oil to the filters rubber gasket, so it wont 'scrunch' up when screwing back on. An option to use the oil filter wrench to tighten it up is not a good idea, it could puncture the metal canister. Using a piece of folded 'coarse' sandpaper then hand pressure only is sufficient to tighten it. Oil is now added, around 4.5 litres, (on a dry engine) or slightly less on a normal fill.
