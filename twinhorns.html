MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages

General maintenance, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

Recently replaced the poor output of the MG's single horn for a set of twin horns, these made a tremendous difference in creating a much more positive and safer sound, Just as good as the ones originally fitted to the old 200. Quick access to the horns, is just to have the steering on full lock, and pull back the wheel arch liner, However working in a tight space to do a proper job is nearly always going to be slightly difficult.

For this project, took the decision to remove the bumper to include other jobs which made it much easier to complete each of them.
These were to include removal, and a good clean out of the wind screen washer tank, bonnet locking mechanism cleaned and greased, and also giving really good access to the cooling systems radiator. 

The windscreen washer tank was drained, cleaned out and the pump filters cleaned from years of gunge resulting in a more powerful screen wash. The radiator fins were eliminated of a build of bugs, using a soft nylon brush, this will slightly increase the vehicles cooling properties.   



Bumper to wing are 4 x10mm bolts, 2 at each side, accessed pulling back the wheel arch liners, for this it will require having the steering on full alternate lock, these 4 bolts are not the easiest to remove, and will be corroded with the possibilty of having to replace them.


Along the bottom edge are 3 x 10mm bolts in the middle, with 6 cross head screws, 3 located at each corner. Along the leading edge are 5 x 10mm bolts. Also needs lower lights system disconnected if wiring is too short for placement of bumper as shown, this was a straight swop, although the horn bracket being very corroded from where its been located, so replaced this with new supplied alloy fitting kit.
