MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages 

General maintenance, cooling and fuel system, cabin and engine fuse box listing, audio remotes, ignition, navigation, vehicle monitoring and colour codes, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

The tailgate wiring loom of the 200/25/ZR also including various other models, do tend to cause plenty of electrical problems, especially in the section of where the wires pass from the body to tailgate, the two small rubber protection sleeves, which are located on either side of the tailgate hinges, are associated with the following. The sleeve on the left carries the water supply tube to rear screen wash, sleeve on the right carries a multitude of wires for window heater element, high level brake light etc, and are packed tightly into the sleeve. so not surprised problems occur. Wires inside the sleeve, are constantly folding over each other every time tailgate is in use, over time they will fray and eventually break, 


Any damaged wires will then create really annoying electrical problems, soldering and insulating a couple of broken wires is not a problem, but if its beyond a really decent repair then a replacement loom is a more better option, loom shown here, was a scrapyard find and a direct fit as all wires were in a very good condition.

Having a multitude of electrical wires all bunched together into a tight space such as the narrow rubber protection sleeve, and with tailgate constantly in operation is nearly always going to be a problem, adding something such as talcumn powder to inside of the sleeve can eliminate some friction, the internal of the sleeve would of course need to be totally dry for this application. Its still always best to first decide if there actually is a problem within the wiring harness, As an example, if just one brake light bulb is out, then its a simple bulb replacement, If both of the brake light bulbs are out, it could be either a wiring fault, or just a blown fuse, both these would be easier options to first resolve.
