MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages

General maintenance, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

Only really serious problem Ive had with the cooling system shortly after getting the one owner ZR in 2010, was having no heat in the cabin. I had replaced the radiator which was very badly corroded due to a previous long term weak, and incorrect coolant mix, new hoses were also added. Using a trial and elimination process, cabin heater control dial was set to hot position, radiator bottom hose detached, and coolant drained off, The first to check were the heater control valve, cabin heater control dial, and then the heater matrix.

The cabin heater controlled coolant valve that is attached to the bulkhead, is nothing more than just a sliding coolant regulator or shut off valve and is operated directly when rotating the cabin's heater dial for hot or cold air, during testing the valve worked as it should..

From inside of the cabin, checked the heater control dial for freedom of movement, this was rotated several times from the hot to cold position, just to confirm that the sheafed steel control rod worked correctly, and operated in a smooth open, and closed operation. Next to check was the possibility of a blocked heater matrix, so with a clean tube attached to the inlet pipe, a light blow through test expelled clean and clear coolant, same with the outlet pipe, no blockages were evident. So a blocked, or in some way damaged cabin heater matrix valve was now suspected.  

The heater matrix valve PCH-003300 basically a SAAB C900 fitment, routes hot coolant which is circulating in the engine, to the cabin heater, an internal fault with the valve restricted this process, so a replacement was now being considered. The valve is supplied with all new coolant hoses already attached for a direct fit, so a good time to replace all the hoses, on these now old cars. An alternative quick fix option to by-pass the valve would be a cheaper way, but this would probably interrupt the K16 specific cooling system flow route.

With a new valve fitted, and coolant hoses attached, the cooling system could now be filled, However taking into account the coolant that was expelled from the heater matrix during my earlier test may create more problems with an air-lock in the cooling system.

Coolant capacity for the 200/25 and ZR is 5.5 litres, this is however quoted only for a dry fill. So with the heater return hose now re-attached, and heater dial still set to the 'hot' position, and with a 50/50 coolant and water mix, then started on a slow filling procedure. A really slow procedure is the key to a good result. After allowing free flowing coolant to emerge for several seconds or more from out of the coolant rail, the 8mm bleed screw bolt was then refitted back, this would be done 'without a break' from pouring in the coolant.


From then on coolant tank will slowly start to fill, during this process, I would always take my fill capacity up to and beyond a maximum level, before screwing back on the coolant cap, then running the engine on tickover only for the next several minutes or so to circulate.

Increased engine revs and radiator hose squeezing was not really necessary during this procedure, the added volume of coolant should search out all areas within the system. Coolant level when checked on a 'cold engine' may need a tad to be syphoned out, although my own preference, to eliminate too many false notifications of the fitted coolant level sensor, has always been to retain at a constant and maximum plus fill level.
