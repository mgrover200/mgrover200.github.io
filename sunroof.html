SUNROOF DAMP HEADLINING ROVER 200/25/ZR

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Noticed recently the front corner of the MG passenger side roof headlining was showing signs of being damp, after a heavy and sustained rainfall water would collect in that area, investigating the problem before winter weather sets in, showed that a build of hard sediment, over the years, had formed within the front nearside drainage tube. A damp headlining is bad news at any time, more so in the winter months.

SEDEMENT IN THE DRAINAGE TUBE

A length of plastic coated curtain wire broke it down while pushing through as shown, the wire wont go through the whole length due to a tight bend in the lower section, pulling back wheel arch liner with steering on full left lock gave easy access to complete the process.

If however the sunroof seal needs to be replaced, tilt the panel and push off the 2 plastic side trims that covers the mechanism to expose the securing bolts there are a total of 3 to each side. Using a T25 star bit, remove all of the 6 bolts, and push out the sunroof panel from inside the vehicle, while a helper retrieves it, better safe than sorry. With glass panel now on a blanket covered flat area, the old seal was removed.

Preperation can now begin, we used a small rotary wire brush attached to the dremel..for all rust areas especially the groove which the seal fits into are cleaned up, other areas were back edge of the glass frame are treated to hammerite smooth black, then applied silicone sealant to the new seal and bonded it to the glass with joint being at the 'rear' of the panel, then left to cure for an hour or so.

SUNROOF PANEL PRIOR TO REMOVAL

Glass sunroof panel tilted, showing one side of the 6 bolts before removal. With the panel removed and waiting for the new seal to bond, the mechanism was treated to a coating of copper ease.   

Before panel is placed back into roof aperture, tilt mechanism needs to be in the 'closed' position, with the glass panel centralised, and level with the car roof...each of the six bolts can be inserted and tightened, testing the roof operation is needed before each of the plastic side trims are fitted back, if any further adjustments are required.

Good preperation and using quality silicone sealant should make this fitting a one off for the life of the vehicle, A new type of seal thats available now has a 'soft close' velvet edge, compared to an older original worn seal, that can grab at the roof opening, lifting it away from the glass panel after years of usage, with some of the older cars still being the original factory fitted ones.

OTHER HEADLINING RELATED PROBLEMS

SLIGHT SAGGING TO REAR OF HEADLINING

This problem has recently affected our 16 year old vehicle, could be age related, most probably the bonding drying out over a period of time, vehicle interior is going to look a shabby if not sorted out. 

The suspect area was directly around the rear passenger roof grab handle, the simple solution of using an hair dryer to heat up the bonding glue, while constantly smoothing out the affected area, has worked so far. Although no signs of damp were evident, we took the choice of removing the roof strip ( shown located ) cleaned out the channel, before applying silicone sealant around each of the securing holes.
