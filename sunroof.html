VARIOUS SUNROOF RELATED ARTICLES FOR 200 25 ZR

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Noticed recently the front corner of the MG passenger side roof headlining was showing signs of being damp, after a heavy and sustained rainfall, investigating the problem before the winter weather. Showed a build of hard sediment, over the years, had formed within the front nearside drainage tube. A damp headlining is bad news at any time, even more so in the winter months.

A length of plastic coated curtain wire broke it down while pushing through, wire wont go the full length in the lower section, pulling back the wheel arch liner gives easier access.

At this stage we decided to replace the ageing sunroof seal, with glass panel tilted, and plastic side trims removed (shown) the six securing bolts were all removed using a T25 star bit, the panel was then pushed out from inside the vehicle while a second person retrieves it, better safe than sorry. With glass panel now on a blanket covered flat area, the old worn seal can now be removed.

Using a small rotary wire brush attached to the dremel..for all rust areas, especially the groove which the seal fits into are cleaned up, other areas were back edge of the glass metal frame treated to hammerite smooth black, then applied silicone sealant to new seal and bonded it to the glass with the joining being at the (rear) of the panel, then left to cure for a couple of hours or so.

Sunroof panel tilted, showing one side of the 6 bolts before removal, with panel removed waiting for the new seal to bond, mechanism was treated to a coating of copper ease.    

Before panel is placed back into roof aperture, tilt mechanism needs to be in the 'closed' position, with the glass panel centralised, and level with the car roof...each of the six bolts can be inserted and tightened, testing the roof operation is needed before each of the plastic side trims were fitted back, if any further adjustments are required.

Good preparation and using quality silicone sealant should make this fitting a one off for the life of the vehicle, A new type of seal thats available now, has a 'soft close' velvet edge, compared to an older all rubber seal, that can grab at the roof opening, lifting it away from the glass panel after years of use.
