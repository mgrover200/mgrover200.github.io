Replacing MG-ZR Ignition Switch  

No dashboard lights and starting issues, we fit new ignition switch 

An annoying problem encountered some time ago, was with a suspected fault within in the sealed unit of the vehicles ignition switch, no dash panel lights, just the sound of the fuel pump being primed for the starting procedure. Only time to get a panel display, was with initiating 'quick and positive' turns of the ignition key, panel lights would then activate, but would only catch on maybe just a couple of times out of several attempts by using this procedure. At this stage, took the best option of checking all of the obvious earthing points and other related items.  

Alternator connections were first checked, along with condition of the 12volt battery, plus a check on all related earthing points, including crimping a new spade connector to the starter motor. However doing this made no difference to the vehicles starting problem.  

To replace the YXB-100350 ignition switch, steering wheel is lowered down, both column covers removed, top part will just prise off, bottom part secured with 3 screws, cabin fuse box lid also needs removing, the switch is attached to bottom of key barrel with two small cross head screws...access to these is slightly difficult, removal of the dial surround which is secured with 4 screws, created an easier working area.

With the new ignition switch fitted, the multi-connector can now be just swopped over. The ignition key barrel that is factory secured with shear bolts to the steering column, did not need to be removed at any time during this whole procedure.

A question often asked, is it ok to oil my ignition key to give it a smoother action when inserted into the ignition key barrel. When the key has been lightly oiled, or given a light smear of graphite grease to both the serated edges, and then inserted into the key barrel, it helps loosen each of the individual key security spring loaded digits within the key barrel, so giving a much more smoother and glide free key action, taking note that this method applied to the 200 and ZR only applies to my own personal experience.
