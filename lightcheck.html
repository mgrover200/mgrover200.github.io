INSTANT NOTIFICATION OF A BLOWN REAR BULB ZR

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Generally doing a check on my lights every week or so, I could not have known when a blown bulb had actually occured during any period from that time, so on deciding that the tail, and especially the brake lights are important for safety and legal requirements, so a bulb monitoring system was now being considered to be created and installed into the MG over next few days, using solid core end glow fibre optic cables.

MONITORING THE REAR BULBS

The optic cables were inserted into rear of the dial panel, to show through directly under the cabin's temp gauge placed in a specific sequence. The outer ones are the brake light bulbs, and inner ones, tail light bulbs. This example shows a blown offside tail light bulb. .

FIBRE OPTIC CABLES INSERTED 

One of the light units, shown removed from vehicle complete with the fibre optic cables inserted after trial and error getting a correct position for a good light output from each of the rear bulbs.

So another low cost safety addition added to the old MG, no electrical wiring, switch or any power supply needed, the visible light emitting from the fibre optics, about the size of a small pin head are clear to view, and not a distraction during night time driving. also with no need for a second person to assist, or an implement to hold down brake pedal while going round to do visual light check's.  

The main aspect of this motoring safety feature as applied to the 2004 MG-ZR. is knowing instantly when a tail or brake light bulb is out, and with an immediate notification to show exactly which one of the blown bulbs that needs to be replaced as it happens, so keeping safe and also driving legal. You never know, who is going to sneak in behind you. Project Design and Installation. (c) 2016.
