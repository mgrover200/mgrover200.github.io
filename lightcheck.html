BRAKE/TAIL LIGHTS MONITORING SYSTEM

SERVICES AND MAINTENANCE ROVER 200/25/ZR

Generally doing a check on my lights every week or so, I could not have known when a blown bulb had actually occured during any period from that time, so on deciding that the tail, and especially the brake lights are important for safety and legal requirements, so a bulb monitoring system was now being considered to be created and installed into the MG over the next few days.

MONITORING REAR BULBS

Precise drilling to accept the cables, was important for a neat finish as shown, Using electric mirror switch for this initial trial project, saved drilling any holes directly into dashboard, of which any mistakes could not have been rectified. 

Using four lengths of solid core end glow small diameter 1.5 mm fibre optic cables. each placed in a specific sequence along the lower edge of the mirror switch, shown above with the two outer ones being the nearside and offside brake light bulbs, and the two inner ones being the nearside and offside tail light bulbs, will notify of a blown bulb, and exactly which one needs to be replaced.     

FIBRE OPTICS FACING EACH BULB

One of the light units, shown removed from vehicle complete with the two fibre optic cables inserted after getting a correct position for a good light output from each of the rear bulbs.

So another low cost safety addition added to the old MG, no electrical wiring, switch or any power supply needed, the visible light emitting from the fibre optics, about the size of a small pin head are clear to view, and not a distraction during night time driving. also with no need for a second person to assist, or an implement to hold down brake pedal while going round to do visual light check's.  

2020 FUTURE PROJECT   

A future and more professional update is being planned to route the four thin optic cables into rear of the dial panel, to show through an unused section directly under cabin's temperature gauge, so giving a more better drivers line of view.

The main aspect of this motoring safety feature as applied to the 2004 MG-ZR. is knowing instantly when a tail or brake light bulb is out, and with an immediate notification to show exactly which one of the blown bulbs that needs to be replaced as it happens, so keeping safe and also driving legal. You never know, who is going to sneak in behind you. Project Design and Installation. (c) 2016.
