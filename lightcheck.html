BRAKE LIGHT BULB MONITORING SYSTEM 2004 ZR

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Generally doing a check on my lights every week or so, I could not have known when a blown bulb had actually occured during any period from that time, so on deciding that the tail, and especially the brake lights are important for both safety and legal requirements, a bulb monitoring system was now being considered to be created and installed into the MG over the next few days.

LIGHT OUTPUT OF THE TAIL LIGHT BULBS

Precise drilling to accept the cables, was important for a neat finish as shown,  Using the electric mirror switch for this project, apart from its suitable location. also saved having to drill any holes directly into dashboard, of which any changes to the original plan or any messed up mistakes can not be rectified, but the mirror switch could be replaced. 

Using four lengths of solid core end glow small diameter 1.5 mm fibre optic cables. each placed in a specific sequence along the lower edge of the mirror switch, and shown above with the two outer ones being the nearside and offside brake light bulbs, and the two inner ones being the nearside and offside tail light bulbs, giving an instant notification of a blown bulb, and exactly which one that needs to be replaced.     

FIBRE OPTICS TO FACE EACH OF THE BULBS

One of the light units, shown removed from vehicle and complete with the two fibre optic cables inserted after getting a correct position for obtaining a good and reasonable light output from each of the tail and brake light bulbs before replacing back into the vehicle.

So another low cost safety addition added to the old MG, no electrical wiring, switch or any power supply needed, the visible light emitting from the 4 fibre optics, about the size of a large pin head are clear to view, and not a distraction during night time driving. No need for a second person to assist, or an implement to hold down the brake pedal while going round to do visual light check's.  

2020 UPDATE BEING CONSIDERED FOR THIS PROJECT  

A future and more professional update is being planned to route the four thin optic cables into rear of the dial panel, to show through this unused section directly under cabin's temperature gauge, so giving a much better drivers line of view for monitoring each active element of both the vehicles tail and brake light bulbs, as illustrated within this small viewing area. 

This feature was taken from an old product, first used some years ago, product name unknown, in which the fibre optics were attached on to the outside of the rear light lens using small sticky pads to register each individual lens area. My own featured project is nothing more than a simple update. The main aspect of this fitting, is to know when a tail or brake light bulb is out, and to show exactly which one that needs to be replaced as it happens, keeping safe and driving legal. You never know who is going to sneak in behind you. (c) 2016.
