CHARCOAL CANISTER PURGE VALVE 200/ZR

Whats the function of the combined EVAP Canister and Purge valve, how does it help with controlling the emissions. why can it be so annoying, to the extent of spoiling the smooth cruising sound of my car with the tapping sound emulating from the purge valve that is attached, although an interchangeable part that sits on to the top of the vehicles charcoal canister.

CHARCOAL CANISTER PURGE VALVE

The main problem on vehicles with 'really excessive' tapping. could be a leak somewhere in the EVAP system, damaged fuel filler cap seal, saturated pellets within the canister, a damaged  missing or a badly worn isolation rubber.

The canister which is located between the fuel filter and coolant tank is fitted with a triangular rubber isolation mount, fixed into a metal bracket on the bulkhead. A poor condition of the isolation rubber, or even a misplaced one, will be a contributing factor how audible the purge valve noise will sound inside of the cabin. 

Basic function of the EVAP system, is creating a vacuum in the fuel tank and fuel lines so fumes can be sucked into the engine to burn, instead of venting to the atmosphere. A damaged fuel filler cap seal wont create much of a vacuum, and forcing the purge valve to activate for periods when maybe its not always required to do so.

How does it work, The purge valve an electrically operated solenoid collects fuel vapors from the fuel tank and stores them in the charcoal canister, fuel vapors are then moved from the charcoal canister to the fuel injector then burned off inside the engine, The purge valve flow is monitored with sensors to determine a precise amount So helping in some way with the vehicles emissions.

The tapping sound given off by the purge valve, can initially be mistaken for sounding like the fuel injectors clicking, high tension leads arcing, or even the sound of a slack tappet giving an obvious cause for concern. Tapping on some purge valves are nothing more than a nuisance, and just about audible for short periods, but not consistant, as fumes are purged from the charcoal canister.
