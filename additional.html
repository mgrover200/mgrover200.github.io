REVIEWING ADDITIONAL PROJECTS 

About features, and driver notifications including some of our own designs

Battery Cover and Tool Tray ZR (c) 2010  

Used for working on small jobs in and around the engine bay, that eliminates shorting the electrics, also keeping your tools handy..    

Detachable Car Ramps 

We have never used the old traditional metal car ramps, they can sometimes slip away. Ours are wood with detachable ramps.    .

Travel Companion for the 200/ ZR (c) 2010 

Contains all or most of the items needed en-route for those days away, fluids spares, tools etc, detachable when not required.

Rover 200 parts and tool store (c) 2004

On weekends away, this at the time, was an ideal storage area, for basic parts, which also incorporated a locking tool box.   

Navigation System 200/ ZR (c) 2008

The secure location of this fitting, is exposed only after removing the false vent cover, can be placed back when not in use.

Garmin Street Pilot i3 from 2006 to 2008

We fitted this reliable battery operated unit to the 200 that was to be in regular use for the next couple of years.

Oil Sump condition 2005 Rover 200

With oil sump removed years of lightly tarnished oil is evident, and expected, but no thick oily gunge, due to regular oil changes.

Twin Horns a much better option for the ZR

Fitting the horns was a straight foreward swop, made easier with the front bumper out of the way, 

Rover 200 electric windows conversion (c) 2005    

Parts used were from our old MK2 Ford Granada, our set-up was to have a direct switched feed when parked up and ignition off. 

Alternator Shield/Cover 200/ZR

Added protection for any off road events, and ideal coverage for driving along motorway 'surface water' conditions.  

Removing the rear wiper arm 200/ZR

Caution where glass is concerned, not the best of jobs, on a stuck wiper arm, with penetrating oil, it can be squeezed off.    

Battery trickle charging on the ZR (c) 2012  

Not quite ready for a new battery, trickle charged from house or garage, with this easy plug in option..  

Brake and rear bulbs checking system ZR (c) 2014

Dash panel notification of a specific blown bulb, which needs to be replaced. You never know who is going to sneak in behind you.

Original items saves on new parts 200/ ZR

Not much you can do with the ignition switch, except to get a new unit, however the door lock mechanism etc, can be re-built.

Centre Armrest for the Rover 200 /ZR

At the time a much sort after item to add to the interior, at a later date in 2010 it was then transferred into the 2004 ZR   

Glove Box-Ashtray-Door Pockets 200/ZR

Using the more 'supple' rover metro boot carpet to line out various interior areas for camera equipment memory cards etc. 

Mobius Dashcams fitted to the ZR

To power up the mobius monitoring system a drivers side dashboard located button switch, activates each of the cameras. 

Rover 200 gearbox oil change in 2004 

Our straight foreward and none messy filling procedure, with oil now running directly into the gearbox, a much better option.

A precise oil level reading on the ZR

The oil level dipsticks are not the best for showing a precise reading, shown is our chosen level, directly after an oil and filter change.
