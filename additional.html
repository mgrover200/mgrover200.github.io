MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages 

General maintenance, cooling and fuel system, cabin and engine fuse box listing, audio remotes, ignition, navigation, vehicle monitoring and colour codes, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

A selection of add-ons applied to the 200, then transferred into the MG at a later date, most sort after at the time was the centre armrest, heated seats were never really considered for the 200's fabric ones, however they were applied to the MG's driver and front passenger, good choice on cold winter mornings. Other more in depth items are shown below, including the most recent, battery trickle charger.

This remote battery trickle charge option shown here, eliminates lugging out the charger, cable extension reel, or having to attach crocodile grips to the battery terminals.With the power connector attached, and not having the bonnet open, especially on those days when its raining, in which case it generally does, made this process, simple and straight foreward, only downside to this, a slight loss of just under 2 amps due to the extra power cable length used.

Although the convenience did make it worthwhile, till a new battery was purchased and finally fitted. Shown above far right is the cycle cable oil injector, part filled with light oil, and now used for easing off vehicles smaller diameter cables, such as bonnet release cable, accelerator, and including most other cable operated choke systems.

Tool Tray, which is featured directly below, is useful for general purposes in and around engine bay, this makes it an ideal area for small hand tools, and also to place spark plugs, coil packs, and other small items on, with no chance of spanners shorting the electrics, while using top of battery, a one piece natural vented design, that slips over battery, protects from oxidation and secure enough to be left in place. Also from this random selection, suspension strut covers shown above far right, are adapted from a couple of sink plungers, purpose of the dome shape enables storage with quick access for various spare bulb applications, for front of vehicles light system, headlights, indicators etc, both sides are stored safe in each of the covers, saves breaking into a journey time to a parts store, just pop the bonnet, replace bulb and go.

Finally from this random selection an alloy semi contoured quick release alternator weather shield, gives some protection during extreme driving, and especially the conditions along rain lashed motorways, sits well against recently painted manifold heat shield.
