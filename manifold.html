MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages

General maintenance, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

Replacing the inlet manifold gasket, it may not always be necessary to do a full coolant drain down, considering that the inlet manifold is more or less the highest point of the engine, in which the coolant tank could just be syphoned out. However if the manifold gasket is being replaced after several years of motoring, then to replenish the engines cooling system, would be an ideal opportunity for doing this at the same time.

Always found the better option when replacing the inlet manifold gasket is not with it just detached from cylinder head while new gasket is fitted, but removed from the vehicle, its not that much more time consuming, makes it easier to work on and better access for cleaning up the mating surfaces of the unit which requires being perfectly clean, Ive also included on this a check on coolant hoses back of engine, and throttle body clean up and an internal polish.

With the bottom radiator hose detached, coolant was drained off. Several vacuum hoses and various connectors to consider during the manifold removal process, most obvious one being the brake servo vacuum hose, others not necessarily in order are the fuel feed and fuel return hoses etc, fuel return rail is attached to underside of manifold and may need new fuel BAU-5325 O rings. 

Multi-connector under throttle body, shown above with locking tab in its open position is ready to be detached. also removed the 2 x 8mm bolts that secures fuel return rail to the manifold. The inlet manifold's 13mm nuts 3 on top, and 4 on the bottom were then slackened off in sequence, and manifold removed. Access to the underside bolts can be slightly difficult to get at, with throttle body removed it was made a lot easier and with good reach in access. 

Prior to fitting the new manifold gasket LKJ 101110 which does not require any sealant..the unit was cleaned internally, which wasnt too bad, some can be slightly oily and gunged up. Not a good combination for an internal space, of mixing a combined flow of fuel and air.

After the mating surfaces of both the inlet manifold and cylinder head were cleaned up and new gasket fitted, a check on the coolant hoses to rear of engine was done, the manifold could then be fitted back into the vehicle,  The cleaned throttle body now with polished butterfly valve, was re-attached, before filling up with fresh coolant, an engine run to check coolant level and for any fuel or water leaks.

The 4 x 8mm throttle body bolts are removed, easier and less of a disturbance with accelerator cable still attached, its safer and not actually going anywhere, on assembly may need new throttle body O ring MKG 100250, with care and attention, this will not always be required, each coilpack connectors are now disconnected, this part of the wiring is withdrawn when removing the inlet manifold.
