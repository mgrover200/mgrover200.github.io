GEARBOX OIL CHANGE 1996 ROVER 200 

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Oil change on the Rover 200 with R.65 gearbox is straight foreward, easilly done working at ground level, to release the 2 x 17mm drain and refill bolts, ring spanners were used this time, the drain bolt is usually magnetised, so may have small  amount of swarf attached, this is normal. After allowing gearbox oil to drain, the bolt was wiped clean and replaced using a new copper washer.

REMOVING THE GEARBOX 17 MM DRAINAGE BOLT

If the oil has never been drained off, it can be assumed both bolts, especially the drain bolt is going to be difficult to remove. each 17mm drain and filler bolts, require being tightened to 25Nm or 18lbf ft torque, or hand tight plus.

LOCATION OF THE 17 MM OIL FILLER/LEVEL BOLT

This gearbox oil change is based on a vehicle parked on level ground,  to achieve a correct oil fill level, is when oil begins to slowly trickle out Oil used in the 200 gearbox is a SAE75W-80W to an API GL5 viscosity, around 1.75 litres up to the correct level.

AN EASIER AND NON MESSY FILLING PROCEDURE

Our filling procedure, is to insert into the filler hole a clear plastic tube attached to a base removed old redex container, and then gradually topped up with new oil running directly into gearbox, eliminates using the messy squeezy pack method.
