MG-Rover Technical and Information Pages 

General maintenance, cooling and fuel system, cabin and engine fuse box listing, audio remotes, ignition, navigation, vehicle monitoring and colour codes, along with various other information of the Rover 200 and MGZR

Doing an oil change on the Rover 200 with R.65 gearbox is straight foreward can easilly be done working at ground level, to release the 2 x 17mm drain and refill bolts, ring spanners were used for this, the drain bolt which is usually magnetised, may have a small amount of swarf attached to it, this in most cases is normal, after allowing gearbox oil to drain off, drain bolt was then wiped clean and replaced including new copper washer.

If the oil has never been drained off previously, it can be assumed both bolts, especially the drain bolt is going to be very tight and difficult to remove, laid under front of the vehicle with foot on ring spanner and a good push will soon resolve that problem. Each of the two 17mm drain and filler bolts, require being tightened to 25Nm or 18lbf ft torque, or hand tight plus, this gearbox oil change is based on a vehicle parked on level ground, and having a standard ride height. Otherwise, a pit or service ramps would have to be another option.

Oil used in the 200 gearbox is a SAE 75W-80W to an API GL5 viscosity, takes around 1.75 litres up to correct level, the filling procedure used was to insert into the filler hole a clear plastic tubing which is attached to a base removed old redex container.This was then gradually topped up with new oil running directly into gearbox. The hands free operation should eliminate using the messy squeezy pack method when trying to complete this process and working in a restricted space. Although using a container underneath gearbox area for oil spillages would be advisable, an amount of 1.75 litres, is for a basic gear box oil change, a dry fill will be more, and need a total of around 2.0 litres.

To achieve the correct fill level during the above, or any other filling process, is when the oil just begins to trickle out of the filler level hole, this featured gear box oil change, was on the Rover 200 in early 2005, and shows at the time that bluecol antifreeze was then being used.


An option taken of not to change the gearbox oil on the 2004 MG-ZR was decided on the basis of most modern gearboxes being 'filled for life' at the factory, the above should fall into this category, Basically doing any future oil changes, may not always result in it being as good or superior in quality, as the original factory filled oil that could now have been drained off.

Monitoring performance of the gearbox over the last 7 years, and with no signs of oil leaks, or any oil staining being evident on any part of the casing, and with a good combination of both smooth and positive gear changes, this option on the vehicle still remains.
