French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Issues with the vehicles roof headlining, this problem recently affected our 18 year old vehicle, could be age related, most probably the bonding of the adhesive had dried out over a very long period of time, suspect area was around the rear passenger roof grab handle.

Although no signs of damp were evident, we took the choice of removing the roof strip, cleaned out the channel and then applied silicone sealant around the area (shown here).

A simple solution of using an hair dryer to heat up the adhesive bonding, while constantly smoothing out the affected area, appeared alright, but only for a short period of time, as we probably expected. We now needed to look at any other low cost ways to solve this issue. 

Our next option, adding an extension hose to a bonding spray can with a 360 degree spray pattern, then to be inserted into a fine razor cut, at the lowest part where the lining sags the worst.

The above solution worked very well indeed, and did not require the time consuming hassle of having to remove any of the associated interior trim to to gain access to actually having to remove the whole headlining roof panel from the vehicle.
