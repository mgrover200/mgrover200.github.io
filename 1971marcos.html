1971 MARCOS 3 LITRE V6 FROM THE 1970's

French German Spanish Dutch Italian Russian

Special request from a family member, featuring ownership of their Marcos during the 1970's. The kit car built fibreglass body,  attached to box frame chassis 3M/5849 and powered by a Ford Zodiac V6 engine. First registered on March 1st 1971, and with leicestershire registeration XNR 778J with parts sourced from Triumph, Vauxhall, MG, Austin, Porsche, Hillman Hunter etc. 

WETHERBY NORTH YORKSHIRE FEBRUARY 1976  

From information supplied, here we have a randomly selected image and most probably from well over 40 years ago, venue shown, was at the Turnpike Motel Wetherby, with this meeting being mostly captured on cine film.

SKIPTON NORTH YORKSHIRE MAY 1976

Another meeting, at the Cross Keys Skipton, the year of the so called heatwave, however this was to be a cold and windy day at this time. Although has an extensive private photo collection.   

Marcos cars were always admired in the early years, from well known names to those who for a period of time actually owned one. Below are a just a few of them taken from a list of which has been compiled over the years, and supplied for this featured page.

Rod Stewart (Singer) before upgrading to Lamborghinis. Paul Stassino (Actor) Mick Robertson (Magpie TV Presenter) John Noakes (Blue Peter) Andy Fairweather Low lead singer of Amen Corner had one commisioned in pink. Simon Park (Musician). 

 

Scott Engel and John Maus of The Walker Brothers fame both owned one each in red and blue, an edition of Record Mirror in our collection from February 1966 shows them looking out of the sunroof while kneeling on the transmission tunnel in their cars.

Roger Moore (Saint TV Series) As a racing driver, drove a white Marcos in an early February 1969 episode of 'The World Beater' this along with an extensive list of well known owners from years gone by. All articles related to marcos cars from 1960's to mid 1992.
